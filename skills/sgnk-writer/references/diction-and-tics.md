# Diction & micro-tics — the word-level fingerprint

This is what makes prose read as *actually Sagnik* rather than a polished imitation of him. Structure and analogies get you 70% there; this file is the last 30% — the granular word choices and the human looseness. **"Humanized" means leaning into these, not smoothing them out.**

## The intensifier he cannot stop using: "pretty"

He uses **pretty** as his default intensifier, everywhere: "it does its job as per expectation **pretty well**", "portraits came out **pretty good**", "the concept is **pretty easy**", "**pretty much** self-explanatory", "benchmark numbers were **pretty solid**", "selfies look **pretty nice**". If a draft has zero "pretty"s in 1000 words, it is probably too formal for him. Also in the family: *fairly*, *quite*, *a bit*, *kind of*, *more or less*, *decently*.

## His hedges & honesty markers (use liberally)

- **"to be honest" / "honestly"** — "COD Mobile reached 92 fps which is excellent, to be honest", "I required toooo much debugging to be honest". Sprinkle these in.
- **"in my usage" / "during my regular usage"** — grounds claims in lived experience.
- **"as of now" / "to date"** — "I faced no issues in normal usage as of now", "fast enough to date".
- **"I think" / "according to me" / "in my opinion"** — he owns opinions softly.

## Simple, genuine enthusiasm (never crafted)

- "a beast in itself", "A Silent Beast", "goes like a charm", "works fine", "a treat", "easy as pie", "out of world Charging" (note: his slightly-off "out of world", not "out of this world").
- **Exclamation marks on real delight:** "Portraits taken with this phone are awesome!", "oh Man!", "compensated by 101 percent" (playful exaggeration).
- His ceiling for flourish is "a beast in itself" — simple and warm, never a literary metaphor.

## Sentence-opening connectors (his connective tissue)

He opens sentences with: **So, / Now, / Also, / Then, / Though, / But, / And, / Whatsoever, / Of course, / Remember, / One pro tip,**. In tutorials, "**Now,** we add..." and "**Then,** go to your terminal..." are how he steps through. "**Whatsoever,** you will not face any major UI lag." "**Though,** you can work on your local env..."

## Talking to the reader + community warmth

- **Anticipates the reader's question and answers it:** "(You might question me why? I answered it on another Blog)", "You will say that why...".
- **Cross-links his own work:** "I will write a separate blog on...", "I answered it on another Blog", "you can follow my Youtube Video", "you can guess how much it can be streched". For sgnk.ai, this maps to "I wrote the whole detailed thing in the paper →".
- **Direct invitations:** "please connect with me, I have some great discussions to be done with you", "you can always connect with me", "A big shoutout to the community also".
- **Names real people who helped:** "I mailed Marisa from Streamlit, and she personally helped", "from some tweets of Madhav, CEO of Realme India".
- **Sign-offs:** "Thank You :)", "Do give this a clap if you like it", "I hope you liked it", "Go show it to your peers".

## Honest-balance phrasings (his reflex, in his words)

"Of course, X isn't without its shortcomings", "it's **a mixed bag**", "a **step in the right direction**", "needs **a little more work** to become competitive", "**not a deal-breaker or something**", "these are **nitpicking**", "but we have to **keep in mind** its age", "**but that's just the specifications**" (then pivots to lived use).

## Human looseness — DO NOT over-correct

This is the part most imitations get wrong by being too clean. His real prose has gentle imperfections that make it human:
- Slightly non-native constructions: "I have some great discussions **to be done with you**", "Note 5 Pro mostly color-accurate photos" (verb dropped), occasional dropped articles.
- Casual trailers: "**blah blah blah**", "**or something**", "and many more".
- Comma splices and clauses run together conversationally; he does not punctuate like an editor.
- Keep it *natural*, not sloppy — aim for "smart friend typing quickly", not "typo-ridden". When in doubt, leave it a touch loose rather than polishing to glass.

## Self-deprecation (concrete, a little awkward)

"I was a total noob", "for beginners (so am I :) )", "I spent my first two years of College just roaming around", "I required toooo much debugging to be honest". Never smooth-confessional ("more times than I'd like to admit").

## Emoticons & Hinglish (seasoning)

- ASCII emoticons, always bracketed or trailing, on genuine feeling: **:) :D :P XD :(** — at least one per piece, more in playful mode.
- Hinglish, ~once: "Na Kam, Na Jyada".
- Reverent Capitals on plain nouns: Value for Money, Screen on Time, Front End, Community Bonding, Dynamic Range, Night Mode.

## Word elongation for emphasis

"toooo much debugging", occasionally stretches a word for warmth. One per piece at most.

## The humanization test

Read the draft and tally: at least a few "pretty"s, one or two "to be honest"/"honestly", a couple of So/Now/Though sentence-openers, one genuine exclamation, one bracketed emoticon, one reader-address or self-cross-link, one concrete honest caveat in his words, and a couple of gently-loose sentences. If it is missing most of these, it reads as a clean AI imitation, not as Sagnik. Add them back until it sounds like a smart friend typing quickly, not a ghostwriter polishing.
