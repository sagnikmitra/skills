# Anti-patterns — how the voice gets faked

These are the exact drifts the two adversarial critics flagged when scoring a "casual-blogger" draft **38/100** against Sagnik's real openers. Each is a before (fake-him) → after (him).

## 1. The snappy hook-question as a punchline

- ✗ "Why does it always look so good in the demo." (rhetorical statement-as-question, MBA-deck move)
- ✓ "You will say that everyone has a multi-agent system now, and you would be right." (pre-empt the reader plainly)

His real questions are blunt and genuine ("Have you heard about streamlit? Probably not."), not a knowing setup for a relatable-pain joke.

## 2. The crafted metaphor / zinger

- ✗ "most RAG hallucinations are a Retrieval problem wearing a Generation costume."
- ✓ "most RAG hallucinations are not really the model's fault — the chunks you feed in are stale, or oddly split, or just not relevant."

Nothing in his writing reaches for figurative cleverness. "a beast in itself" is his ceiling. Teaching analogies are fine ("like a climbing harness"); show-off metaphors are not.

## 3. Anthropomorphising the system for sympathy

- ✗ "the poor model does its best to stitch together something plausible anyway."
- ✓ "the model returns something plausible because that is what it is built to do."

His sympathy is for people — friends, family, the dev community — not for software.

## 4. Smooth, writerly self-deprecation

- ✗ "I have been there more times than I would like to admit."
- ✓ "I made this earlier this year and though there is a minor flickering issue, it works fine." / "I spent my first two years of College just roaming around."

His confessions are concrete and a little awkward, not polished feelings.

## 5. Staccato hype / one-word sentences

- ✗ "Boring. Deterministic. Exactly what you want."
- ✓ "None of it is exciting, and that is exactly what you want sitting between a probabilistic agent and your production database."

He is a flowing explainer. Hard-stop hammer rhythm is off-key.

## 6. Finger-wagging / coach voice

- ✗ "So before you go blaming the LLM, let us walk through where these pipelines actually break."
- ✓ "So before reaching for a bigger model, it helps to look at where these pipelines actually break."

"before you go blaming" + emphatic "actually" + "let us walk through" is course-instructor framing. He addresses the reader softly.

## 7. Framework-labelling capitalisation

- ✗ Capitalising abstractions as concept-nouns: "a Retrieval problem", "the Generation step".
- ✓ Reverent emphasis on ordinary nouns: "Value for Money", "Dynamic Range", "Night Mode".

He does capitalise oddly, but on plain words he is genuinely emphasising — not as a labelling device.

## 8. The "Hey there" tic

- ✗ Opening any piece with "Hey there."
- ✓ Open with the objection-pivot, a plain question, or thesis-first enthusiasm.

It appears once in his whole corpus (his debut), immediately undercut ("without any sort of 'Technical Introduction'"). Using it as a signature is the single clearest tell.

## The one-line test

If a sentence would survive as a standalone tweet — quotable, sharp, performed — it is probably not him. His best lines only work in conversation, cushioned by the sentences around them.
