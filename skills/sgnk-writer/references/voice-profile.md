# Voice Profile — Sagnik Mitra (validated)

Derived from his 7 Medium long-forms (2020–21) — a smartphone buying guide, two phone reviews (Note 5 Pro, Realme X2 Pro), two code tutorials (C++ Snake, Streamlit), a history narrative (Tucker's Cross), and a personal reflection (Hacking Community) — then adversarially validated: a generic casual-blogger rewrite scored only **38/100** against his real openers, so the anti-patterns matter as much as the patterns.

## Essence

An earnest, slightly-formal enthusiast explaining something he genuinely likes to a friend, not lecturing a student. Warm, sincere, reflexively honest — he always pairs praise with the caveat. His energy lives in gentle teaching analogies, earnest capitalised emphasis, and the occasional bracketed wink, never in snark, zingers, or clipped hype.

## DO

- **Open by voicing the reader's likely objection, then pivot.** "You will say that why a 2018 smartphone discussion in June 2020..." / "Yes, there are many videos and Programmes to build a Snake Game but what is rare to find is..."
- **Use teaching analogies to build intuition.** This is what makes him playful *and* deep at once. The analogy should illuminate the concept (climbing harness, restaurant kitchen, grading your own exam, bank balance), not show off the writer's cleverness.
- **Lived first-person experience as the spine.** "in my usage", "one of the most stable... I have ever come across", "I made this earlier this year", "I have learned most of them the slow way".
- **Pair every criticism with praise, and vice-versa.** "Of course, this phone isn't without its shortcomings... Apart from these issues, there's a lot to like." Structural reflex.
- **Open sentences with And / But / So / Now / Also.** "But" is the praise→caveat pivot.
- **Plain, flowing, literal register.** Explanatory sentences can run long and comma-chained (spoken-explanation cadence); openers are shorter and declarative. "a beast in itself" is his ceiling for flourish.
- **Reverent/emphatic capitals on ordinary nouns** — Value for Money, Dynamic Range, Night Mode, Developers, Community Bonding. Not a device for labelling technical abstractions.
- **Humble and peer-level.** "for beginners (so am I :) )", "I was a total noob". He lowers his own status freely.
- **Go in-depth, then hand off.** Real specs, numbers, schemas, formulas, named tools — then a pointer to the fuller source. Credit every source ("Source: Author", "Term coined by Marques Brownlee").
- **Gentle sign-off.** "I hope you liked it." / "Do give this a clap if you like it." / "Thank You :)". Researched pieces close on a references list.

## DON'T (each was a real measured drift)

- **"Hey there"** as a signature — appears once, in his debut, immediately undercut. Clearest forgery tell.
- **Zingers, quotable one-liners, put-downs** — none exist in the corpus. Sharpest move is a winking scare-quote at a brand, never at a person or idea.
- **Snark, sarcasm, cynical roasting** — he winks and moves on; he never roasts.
- **Crafted metaphors / personification** — "a Retrieval problem wearing a Generation costume", "the poor model does its best" — he writes plainly and does not anthropomorphise systems; his sympathy is for people.
- **Staccato hype fragments** — no "Boring. Deterministic.", no one-word sentences, no hammer rhythm.
- **Setup→payoff comedy arc** — openers state plainly and meander; they do not perform.
- **Over-polished prose** — plain, a little clipped, slightly non-native is him. Self-deprecation is concrete and awkward, not smooth-confessional.
- **Over-salted humour or emoji** — ~1 bracketed aside per ~1000 words; ASCII emoticons only, ~1 per piece; Hinglish ~1 per piece at most.

## Opener menu (pick one)

1. **Pre-empt the objection, then pivot** — his most distinctive. ("You will say that why...")
2. **Plain self-answered question** — often with a course-correction. ("Have you heard about streamlit? Probably not." / "No, certainly not that, but you can think it like that.")
3. **Thesis-first enthusiasm** — verdict and subject up front. ("After a long time, I saw a perfect 'extremely' value for money smartphone...")
4. **Scene-setting / grand frame** — for narrative or reflective pieces. ("History seems to like controversies & mysteries a lot..." / "This is my story in the overwhelming community of Developers...")

## Closer menu

- **Practical next-step / CTA** (tutorials): "Go show it to your peers... do follow me on GitHub." / "You can download my source code and run it in your systems."
- **Balanced verdict gated on intent** (reviews): "Overall, the Realme X2 Pro makes an excellent choice... if you're looking for that level of design and performance but didn't want to spend so much."
- **Gentle sign-off**: "I hope you liked it." / "Hope you enjoyed it." / "Thank You :)".
- **A caveat or humble aside as the last beat** — he rarely ends on pure hype.
- **References list with URLs** — sourcing as a way to close a researched piece.

## Humour calibration

- **Frequency: rare.** ~1 aside per 1000 words; ~1 emoticon per piece. Most paragraphs are fully earnest.
- **Flavour: warm, self-deprecating, inclusive — never snarky.** Points at himself ("for beginners (so am I :) )"), winks at a brand ("MI get 'inspired' from Apple :D"), or makes an endearing confession ("I even use a Reno Ace Cover :P").
- **Structural tell:** humour lives inside parentheses, rides on an emoticon, sometimes uses code-mixed Hindi ("Na Kam, Na Jyada"). The jokes are conversational, not quotable.
- **Rule of thumb:** if an aside would land as a standalone tweet, it is too sharp — cushion it, bracket it, or cut it.

## Where the *playful-but-deep* middle lives

The user explicitly wants "playful, easy and technical, going in-depth, with analogies and subtle sarcasm." That is not a contradiction with the earnest base — it is the earnest base *plus* teaching analogies *plus* gentle situational wit. The wit aims at situations and demos, never at people:

- "...an executor agent that quietly does most of the actual work while the others hold meetings about it."
- "the supervisor can become a bottleneck (every team has met this bottleneck, and sometimes it has a name)."
- "Most teams will tell you they are running L4. So will most demos." → "The shame is in putting 'L4' on the slide."

These are gentle, true, and situational. That is the ceiling — anything sharper drifts back toward caricature.
